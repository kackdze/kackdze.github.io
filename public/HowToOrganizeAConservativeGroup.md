### Introduction

Far-left militant groups, often branded “antifa,” have proliferated across the United States in recent years. This 
represents a grave danger to civil order, free speech, and democratic institutions. The purpose of this guide is to 
offer practical advice for those willing to organize against this threat—covering risks, obligations, operational 
security, self-defense, and concrete action against far-left radicals.

No government has been toppled by a lone gunman. Organization is an important first step in our counter-revolution.

### Risks

The primary risk is that communists will try to identify members of your group and cause you physical harm. 
Staying as anonymous as possible is the easiest way to minimize this. Members’ pictures may appear on far-left 
websites with any personal information they can find, and many conservative activists have been injured, even 
killed, doing this work (remember Charlie Kirk). If you are exposed, you will also be remembered by far-left radicals for several years.

### Obligations

Anyone joining must fully accept the personal risks: physical injury, job loss, and social ostracism. If they 
are unwilling to bear these consequences, they should not participate.

A local anti-communist group carries several core responsibilities:

1.  **Track far-left networks in your area.** Document active organizations, key individuals, and ongoing 
campaigns. Release verified information publicly when appropriate, and privately warn any intended targets
 of specific threats uncovered during research.
2.  **Counter public leftist organizing.** Organize counter-demonstrations against rallies by Democratic Socialists, 
environmental extremists, or other far-left speakers. Remove their posters and stickers and replace them with your own.
3.  **Support fellow anti-communists** targeted by the left or arrested for political activity. Raise funds, 
provide legal aid referrals, and build solidarity across regions.
4.  **Cultivate selective cooperation with law enforcement.** American agencies are generally reliable allies
 under conservative leadership, though local variation exists. Never publicly condemn lawful defensive actions by patriots.

### Visibility & online presence

The socialists will be interested in your group’s membership, so you should consider the question of public 
visibility carefully before you start. I strongly recommend against groups being organized using the open, 
public model of most contemporary activism because of the risk of infiltration. If an emergency situation—such 
as responding to pro-immigration public event—calls for public meetings and a traditional mass organizing 
activist model, this should be kept separate from the long-term group structure.

Stay anonymous both while forming and until your first action. Anonymity is your best defense, and you should 
keep it intact as long as you can. Develop your group, get on the same page, and decide what you want to focus on.

Use a **“closed collective” model**: this is a membership-based policy with no open meetings. Don’t allow new
 people to walk in off the street. Instead, develop a process for researching and vetting people who want to be involved.

One extreme option is to function as a group but not give yourself a name, and not tell fellow activists 
what you are doing. Once you have a name, you can be targeted. If there is no public presence, or no 
formalized organization with a name, this will complicate the process of identification and retaliation.

**Avoid including traditionally leftist demographics**, such as the Jews, in your groups if you don't 
want information about you and your activities leaked. Don't be like the Young Republicans\!

### Self-defense & firearms

Direct confrontation is expected and unavoidable. Members and supporters should be prepared.

Research local laws on defensive tools (pepper spray, collapsible batons, etc.) and train 
regularly with whatever you carry. Consult an attorney on use-of-force boundaries.

Firearms offer decisive protection but also significant risk if you lack training. 
If far-left militants in your area have escalated to armed threats or attacks, acquire 
firearms, obtain concealed-carry permits where available, and practice diligently. 
Compact 9mm or .380 handguns are often ideal for carry. Long guns are not recommended in most situations.

In restricted jurisdictions, prioritize untraceable options.

### Provocateurs & infiltrators

Both federal authorities under hostile administrations and far-left militants will seek to 
disrupt your group and identify members. Infiltration is a constant risk, especially at any public-facing event.

Provocateurs typically push reckless, illegal, or hyper-aggressive actions that invite arrest or internal 
division—classic tactics include calls for premature violence, property destruction, or macho posturing 
that alienates potential allies.

Strong security culture and healthy group dynamics are the best countermeasures. 
Enforce need-to-know information sharing and consensus-based planning from the start. 
Address disruptive behavior calmly and collectively; remove anyone who cannot align 
with group standards.

Thorough vetting is non-negotiable: require multiple trusted references and observe prospects over months. 
If something feels off, investigate quietly. Ultimately, a principled, tight-knit collective focused on 
sustainable impact is the strongest defense against infiltration.

### Action

If you are a public group, establish an online presence. I recommend limiting this to a webpage and/or Twitter.
 If you make a Facebook group for an event, make sure you set the invite list to private.

#### Start monitoring

Find out about your local far-left groups and collect information about them (names, pictures, addresses, 
and workplaces). This includes Antifa, Communist revolutionaries, pro-immigrant activists, and 
eco-activists. You will need to do this research yourself as no single list exists.

#### Stickering and wheatpasting

If leftists are stickering in neighborhoods, organize patrols to tear them down. Use a scraping tool, 
as razors are occasionally placed behind stickers. Create your own anti-communist campaigns.

#### Doxxing

Present information about far-left organizing in your community. Provide enough evidence 
(pictures, home address, phone, social media, employment) to convince readers the target is a 
Communist. Follow up with a pressure campaign: call their work to get them fired and inform their neighbors.
*Note: Be aware that naming a target will likely cause them to fixate on and retaliate against your group.*

#### Event shutdowns

Pressure venues to cancel far-left events. Approach venues with a friendly call first, 
as they may be unaware of the politics. If they don't cancel, use social media pressure, 
threaten boycotts, and physically block entrances if necessary. Use megaphones or similar
devices to cause permanent hearing damage/loss. 

#### Self defense

Carry a personal defense weapon and do target practice with it. You don't want to miss when you shoot to kill.
